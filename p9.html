The Story

The townspeople of San Jose called it “The Camelot House” for in the center of the ancient manor stood a round table. It stood, high and mighty, in a clearing of the city, its windows boarded and covered in unchecked cobwebs. A once magnificent manor was dilapidated, and uninhabited. Whispers of an old raggedy caretaker spread through San Jose, though no one really believed he existed. Some even speculated he was the ghost of the wizard Merlin. Most granted these theories as urban legends or conspiracy theories, refusing to believe in unproven myths. That was until the great flood. It started just outside the Camelot House. Water shot out from the terrestrial orb that was Earth lifting the house into the air. The old man hobbled to the porch as he watched, a look of almost hope evident across his face. As each droplet touched the edges of the house, it began to metamorphize. Creaky wood turned to hardened stone, as boarded-up windows became arches of stained glass. The main door of hinges barely hanging by a spider's web transformed into a large drawbridge as the stories of the original house molded into towers touching the heavens. The Camelot House was no longer, in its place stood the great Camelot castle, thought to have been lost in the middle ages. The old man, no, he showed no signs of age stepped outside the newly formed drawbridge looking up and seeing his once beloved Camelot. A tear ran down his cheek as a tap on his shoulder caused him to turn around. A woman, hair flowing like the waters of Avalon itself held a talisman in hand, reaching out to the man. “Camelot has been reborn, my love. You deserve a second chance. From the depths of Avalon, use this wisely.” The Lady of the Lake whispered into the man's ear. “Only those who are truly worthy to rule Camelot once more may wear it. Any other who tries will corrupt with its power.” The Lady of the Lake continued before disappearing into the waters below. Merlin looked down at the talisman now sitting in the small of his palm. Embossed with the Pendragon crest, he placed the bracelet in front of the castle. “A second chance,” Merlin whispered as he turned back to his beloved home.

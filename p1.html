Part 2

1. The first quote I chose is from a childhood hero of mine, Doctor Who. The quote is as follows, “We all change, when you think about it. We're all different people all through our lives. And that's ok, that's good, you gotta keep moving, so long as you remember all the people that you used to be.” The quote expresses the constant evolution of people and society as a whole. Personally, this quote defines how my art has grown over the years, primarily through the medium. I started out with traditional mediums, only to create art with a computer, something I would have never dreamed of. Despite my change in medium, I always start my art process with paper and pencil, sketching out my ideas and going through my process as if it were a drawing. Remembering my origins in the art world gives me a solid foundation to grow from.
2. The next quote I chose comes from Vanessa Marianna, an art dealer in the world of Marvel’s Daredevil. She expresses that “there's something very intimate in experiencing art through someone else's eyes.” I found it apt for my art practice as I have worked with many types of interaction design which is directly related to perspectives of art. In any interactive media, my personal pieces will be experienced through millions of other eyes. This expresses not only myself but challenges that expression with the viewpoints of others.
3. This final quote comes from my new fixation with Dream from Netflix’s Sandman. Dream of the Endless explains how “when the waking world leaves you wanting and weary, sleep brings you here to find freedom and adventure, to face your fears and fantasies in Dreams and Nightmares.” To me, this quote relates to the subconscious. I always struggle with coming up with ideas in art. Once the idea is found, my artistic process flows; however that initial idea is always a struggle. This leads me to jump to conclusions about the first idea I have. Instead, I would take some of Morpheus’ advice. His quote signifies the realm of dreaming and the mulling of ideas through the subconscious. I would love to be able to explore ideas in my mind and, instead of immediately acting on them, allowing my subconscious to really understand and explore them.
4. As someone interested in the interaction design of gaming, this reimagination of the classic game Asteroids displays my interest in the field. As said before, I love working with art that can be experienced through a different set of eyes, and games allow you to do exactly that. To create this game, I started with sketches of each frame, as with any of my art pieces, then translated them into JavaScript through the p5.js libraries.
